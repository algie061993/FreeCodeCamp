Recipe: Pork Sinigang

Pork Sinigang, or Sinigang na Baboy, is a traditional Filipino sour soup known for its savory and tangy flavor profile. It is typically made with pork, a variety of vegetables, and a souring agent like tamarind. This dish is beloved for its comforting taste and is often served with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • tamarind (Sampaloc)
  • Radish (labanos)
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Long beans (sitaw)
  • Eggplant (talong)
  • Okra
  • Water spinach (kangkong) or other leafy greens like mustard greens
  • Green chili peppers (siling haba), added for a mild kick or simply for their aroma

Instructions

  1. Boil Pork: Place pork in a pot, cover with water, bring to boil, skim foam, then simmer until tender (45-60 min).
  2. Add Aromatics: Add quartered onions and tomatoes; simmer 10-15 minutes.
  3. Introduce Sourness: Stir in tamarind soup mix (or fresh tamarind pulp); simmer 5 minutes.
  4. Add Hardier Veggies: Add radish and green chilies (if using); simmer 5-7 minutes.
  5. Add Softer Veggies: Add long beans and eggplant; simmer 3-5 minutes.
  6. Add Final Veggies: Add okra and kangkong; cook 1-2 minutes until just wilted.
  7. Season: Season generously with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  8. Serve Hot: Ladle into bowls and serve immediately with rice.
